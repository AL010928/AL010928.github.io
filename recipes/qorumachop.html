Ingredients

  • Hand-pulled noodles (but you can replace this with any other chewy noodles)
  • Onions (sliced)
  • Minced garlic
  • Red pepper, sliced
  • 500g beef strips
  • Chinese chives
  • Handful of dried chili
  • Soy sauce
  • Dark soy sauce
  • Cumin powder
  • Ketchup
  • Oyster sauce
  • Chili bean sauce Ë±ÜÁì£ÈÜ¨ (can be found at Chinese grocery stores)
  • MSG (oh yes ü§åüèº)

Instructions

  1. Soak the dried chilies in water for 10-15 minutes, and cut the onion and pepper into strips.
  2. Marinate the beef with black pepper, white pepper, one spoon of soy sauce, one spoon of dark soy sauce, and cornstarch. Mix well, then drizzle with olive oil and leave in fridge to marinate for 15 minutes.
  3. Boil the noodles for 5 minutes (or however long it takes for them to cook), then wash in cold water to stop the cooking.
  4. Heat up some oil in a HOT (!!) wok, stir-fry the beef until browned, then set aside.
  5. Add the sliced garlic and the white parts of Chinese chives, and cook until fragrant. Add a spoon of chili bean sauce, dried chilies, onions, and pepper.
  6. Add one spoon of the following: ketchup, soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, and a pinch of white pepper and MSG. Add cumin to taste.
  7. Add the beef, the noodles, and Chinese chives. Cook until everything is coated in this amazing sauce...
  8. Top with sesame seeds, and serve piping hot with a nice bowl of rice (or 3, as this dish always makes me overeat).
