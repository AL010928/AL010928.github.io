2024/06/29: A Simple Model for Online Message Discipline

Same vibe as building a model using Taylor Swift ticket sales to predict next quarter inflation.... read with caution.

Introduction

The idea that I'm concerned with is this: In political discussions, leftists should moderate their speech to appear less off-putting and less provocative, in the hopes of building an effective coalition. It is in the genre of respectability in politics: for example, abolishing prisons seems like a radical idea. Therefore if you start with a full speech filled with vocabularies such as abolition, exploitation, disproprotionally affecting people of colour, then to the uninitiative ear this will appear off-putting right away and hence stands in the way of effective coalition building.

I'm not a fan of this genre, and there are at least 2 reasons, I'll focus on the last.

Some preliminary notes before I go in. I think that my arguments against speech discipline apply for only some people. Specifically, it applies to regular people discussing and talking in the context of social media/in real life. It does not apply to people 1)with a platform, nor does it apply to 2)1-1 conversations where there is already established trust between the parties. Further, it will not apply to people who do not believe in the implicit assumption of the original argument, which is that your ultimate goal is leftist victories. This will rule out, for example, journalists, whose role, you can argue, is properly distinct from seeking partisan political victories. This caveat aside, here are the reason why I think that speech discipline is a bad idea.

Argument

The first reason: Too much Machiavellian playing with expression, action, and preferencees can be at odds with how we should conduct in a democracy. It is perfectly possible that the above genre leads to democratic preference aggregation that do not take into account the full range of sentiments and knowledge that is within the society. I believe that this is true to some extent, but I'll leave my thinking on this aside for now.

The second reason: Suppose that this is the situation faced by our typical postmodern neo-Marxist serpent: they come across a discourse where they are faced with the decision of speaking their political minds, or moderating their speech.

Assumption 1: In general, our protagonist prefers speaking their minds to not speaking their minds.

Assumption 2: For simplification (though nothing depends upon this simplification), our protagonist would rather the left win (more supporters for a bill, popular opinion, what not). But if moderating their speech would change a result from losing to winning, they would rather moderate their speech.

Assumption 3:As an individual, they will not be able to change other people's opinion of the Left as a whole (critiques of them will always other people to protray as unreasonable). Now it turns out that our results depends significantly on this, but expansion+justification later.

Hence, we have a decision model as follows:

Game Matrix: Online Message Discipline
Left Win Left Lose
Speaking Mind 4 2
Message Discipline 3 1

A simple dominance argument would suffice to show that it is always better to speak your minds. As mentioned before, the results turn on the assumption that you cannot change the outcome based on your conducts online. I beleive that for most of us, this is true.

But let's now consider a more complicated scenario: what if you are able to influence the results? Again, I do not think that this is true for most people, but you would have some sort of influecen if you are, let's say, someone with a platform. I'll call this act-state dependence: one's action affect the probability of one world state happening over the other.
