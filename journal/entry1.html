Nov 18st, 2020

我的小猫很普通

网络上可以找出一千只

同款印花的他

路上遇到几只泼妇猫

看起来都很像他的妈妈

我的小猫不常说话

饿了也不说话 被欺负了不说话

只有听到我摁打火机的“咔嚓”时

小猫才会喵喵叫

他也想摁摁我的头

可惜我只会叹气 不会发光

我的小猫来自一个神秘的地方

他第一次见到我

就把自己柔软的肚子

大大方方地露出来

他好像忘了鼻子上还有伤口

忘了门外每天走过的十只

仗势凌人的狗

我的小猫只有在害怕的时候

才会把头埋进我的臂弯

和平的日子里

他只想跟我呆在一个房间

追着啤酒拉环蹦蹦跳跳

偶尔想起身边还有一个人类

也像它一样沉默

就过来嗅一嗅

这个月亮欣赏不来追琢的乐趣

怪不得那么不活泼

小猫每天都吃同一款猫粮

呆在同一个地方

却从不厌倦生命

也不向往远方

就像这个春天到临的麻雀

他们的年岁

只相差两个月

April 1st, 2021

There is a sense of service when tending to plants and animals. In those moments, the self dissolves a bit. Focusing entirely on them—providing them with nutrients, touching them, helping them grow into thriving beings. At the beginning of the year, I brought a small leafy plant from Granville island. For a while, I did not take care of it. But recently, I noticed that its original pot culd no longer contain it. Though no one had tended to it carefully, it still grew. I think this is the true nature of life—resilient, filled with vitality.

So, I began to care for it more attentively.

May 1st, 2021

Last night, I finally was able to fall asleep without fear. For nearly six months it troubled me, keeping me awake. But last night I finally made some progress with it. I can't truly overcome or conquer something that does not exist. Fear (in this particular case) is merely a creation of the mind. And if the mind can create fear, then why can't it create something filled with understanding? So last night, I fell asleep in understanding.
