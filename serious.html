More Serious Stuff

I studied health economics at LSE, where I use quantitative methods to better inform healthcare decisions. I'm now working at UNODC in the Access to Controlled Medicines team, as well as working at a health economics consultancy. This is a sample of my current and past projects:

  • State transition Markov cost-effectiveness analysis of ribociclib plus endorcrine therapy in early breast cancer treatment.
  • Instrumental variable analysis: maternal education and child health in Ethiopia.
  • Systematic literature review and mata-analysis on the Hospital Frailty Score
  • Scoping review on polypharamcy and opioid use
  • Joint UNODC-UNIDO proposal on developing local pharmaceutical manufacturing capcities in Africa (18 million project)
  • Expert meeting and facilitation of a conference on the topic of clinical research development and regulation of psychedelic assisted therapy (MDMA, Ketamine, Psylicybin etc)
